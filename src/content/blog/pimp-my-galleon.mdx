I'd been sailing the same waterlogged disgrace for eleven years. The *Soggy Regret*. Three holes below the waterline I kept plugged with a combination of prayer and one extremely committed crab. The mainsail was a bedsheet I stole from a tavern in Tortuga, and it still had the tavern's name embroidered on it, which made surprise attacks awkward because the enemy always knew exactly where I'd been drinking.

So when the bloke from MTV rowed up alongside and asked if I wanted my ship pimped, I said aye before he'd finished the sentence. I'd have said aye to most things at that point. Morale was low. The crab had quit.

## The Reveal

They blindfolded me and walked me down to the harbour, which is a lot of trust to put in a pirate with one good eye and a peg leg. When they pulled the blindfold off I wept openly, and not just because of the eye situation.

The host - a towering legend in a leather coat who I am legally not allowed to name but who absolutely used to be a rapper - spread his arms wide and bellowed the words that would change my life.

"YO DAWG. We heard you like plunder. So we put a treasure chest INSIDE your treasure chest, so you can hoard while you hoard."

I did not fully understand this. I still don't. But the smaller chest was lined with red velvet and had a little light that came on when you opened it, and I have not stopped opening it since.

## What They Did To My Beautiful, Terrible Ship

Where to even begin. The transformation was so complete that my own first mate drew his cutlass on me thinking I was a wealthy nobleman come to be robbed. Which, to be fair, is the dream.

**The cannons.** They kept all twelve, which I appreciated, but they wrapped each one in chrome and installed something called a "subwoofer" in the gun deck. Now when I fire a broadside it plays a bass drop. The first time we engaged a Royal Navy frigate, the captain surrendered immediately. Not out of fear. Out of respect.

**The figurehead.** My old one was a mermaid that had lost her face to a storm in '09, so for a decade my ship's spirit guide was a faceless wooden woman lunging out of the fog at people. Children cried. They replaced her with a fully animatronic kraken that lights up at night and, for reasons no one can explain to me, occasionally winks.

**The crow's nest.** They put a hot tub in it. A hot tub. Forty feet up. My lookout, Big Davey, has not come down in six days. He says the view is incredible and the water stays warm and he has everything he needs. We pass him biscuits on a rope. He has stopped looking out for danger entirely, but honestly his quality of life has never been higher and I won't take that from him.

**The hull.** Spinning rims. On a boat. I asked how that was supposed to help and the host put his hand on my shoulder, looked deep into my eye, and said, "It's not supposed to help. It's supposed to be FRESH." And then a man with a clipboard wrote down everything we'd said for the cameras.

## The Practical Concerns I Raised And Was Gently Talked Past

I did mention, once, quietly, that what I primarily needed was for the ship to not sink. I have a recurring problem with the ship sinking. It is, you might say, my core issue with the ship.

They heard me out. They nodded. They installed a mini fridge stocked with rum and a flatscreen that plays a continuous loop of a fireplace, which is a bold choice on a wooden vessel surrounded by water and gunpowder.

The three holes are still there. They put a beanbag chair over the biggest one. When we take on water now we just sort of float higher in our seats and call it a "wet lounge." The crab has not returned but I respect that it left on its own terms.

## The Maiden Voyage

We sailed her out of the harbour at dawn and I have never felt so alive. The kraken winked at a passing fishing boat. The cannons dropped the bass. Big Davey waved down at us from his hot tub like a king surveying his realm, which up there, he genuinely is.

We did not catch a single ship to plunder, because the spinning rims create so much drag that we now travel at roughly the speed of a thoughtful walk. A merchant vessel we were chasing simply rowed away from us, slowly, while we watched, the bass still dropping pointlessly into the empty sea.

But you know what? I don't even care.

Because as the host said to me before he disappeared back to wherever legends go between episodes - he leaned in close, the chrome cannons gleaming behind him, and he told me something I'll carry to my grave, which at this rate of sinking is probably Tuesday:

"Dawg. A pirate's true treasure was the *vibe* all along."

I have a hot tub in the sky and a chest inside my chest and a winking kraken and absolutely no way to make a living anymore.

## Six Months Later

People ask how it's going, and I tell them the truth: it is going beautifully, in the way that a sunset is beautiful right before it gets dark and cold and you remember you are alone in the open ocean with no money.

The fireplace loop has never once caught fire, which I now regard as the single greatest stroke of luck of my career. Big Davey came down on day nine, but only to refill the hot tub, and then he went straight back up. We have an arrangement. He is technically still my lookout. He has not looked at anything that wasn't directly above him since spring.

The kraken developed a fault in a thunderstorm and now winks continuously, with both eyes, which the crew has decided is less a malfunction and more a lifestyle. We call it Barnaby. Barnaby has more followers in the port towns than I do. Children who once cried at my faceless mermaid now beg their parents to row out and see him. I have, against every instinct in my pirate body, started charging admission.

And that, in the end, is how the *Soggy Regret* finally turned a profit. Not by plunder. Not by cannon, chrome or otherwise. By being, undeniably, the freshest thing ever to slowly take on water in this harbour. We don't raid anymore. We do tours. The bass still drops. The rims still spin, when the current's kind. And every so often a Royal Navy captain sails past, sees us, and salutes, because some things you simply cannot fight.

Best decision I ever made.

*The Soggy Regret has been renamed the Soggy Magnificent and is currently for sale, partially, the parts above the waterline. Serious inquiries only. Beanbag chair included. Barnaby not included. Crab not included.*
